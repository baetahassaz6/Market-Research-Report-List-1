<p><p>The Polyvinyl Alcohol (PVA) fibers market is primarily divided into two types: filament fibers and staple fibers. Filament fibers are continuous strands that offer enhanced strength and flexibility, making them suitable for applications like textiles and industrial components. In contrast, staple fibers are shorter lengths that resemble traditional textile fibers, often used in non-woven fabrics and consumer goods. Both types provide unique properties for various applications, including water resistance and chemical stability, catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>
