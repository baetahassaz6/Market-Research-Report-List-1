<p><p>The handheld monocular market is segmented into two primary types: manual focus and fixed focus. Manual focus monoculars allow users to adjust the lens for optimal clarity based on their subject distance, providing versatility for various viewing conditions. In contrast, fixed focus monoculars are designed with a preset focus range, enabling quick observation without the need for adjustments. This simplicity makes fixed focus models ideal for casual users, while manual focus models cater to those seeking precision in their optical experiences.</p></p>
