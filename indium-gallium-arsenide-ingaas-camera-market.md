<p><p>The Indium Gallium Arsenide (InGaAs) camera market is divided into two primary technologies: cooled and uncooled. Cooled InGaAs cameras employ thermoelectric or cryogenic cooling systems to reduce sensor noise, enhancing sensitivity and performance in low-light conditions, making them ideal for applications like spectroscopy and scientific research. In contrast, uncooled InGaAs cameras operate at ambient temperatures, offering a more compact and cost-effective solution for applications such as industrial inspection and surveillance, though typically with slightly lower sensitivity.</p></p>
