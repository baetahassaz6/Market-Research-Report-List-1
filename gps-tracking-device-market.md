<p><p>The GPS tracking device market comprises two primary types: satellite and cellular. Satellite GPS tracking relies on signals from satellites orbiting the Earth, providing global coverage and high accuracy, ideal for remote areas. Conversely, cellular GPS tracking utilizes existing mobile networks to transmit location data, making it more suitable for urban environments where cellular signals are strong. Both types cater to diverse applications, from personal tracking and fleet management to wildlife monitoring, each with its distinct advantages and limitations.</p></p>
